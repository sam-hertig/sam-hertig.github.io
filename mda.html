Multidomain Assembler (MDA) for UCSF Chimera

During his time as a postdoc at UCSF, Sam Hertig et al. developed an extension for the open-source molecular graphics program UCSF Chimera to facilitate modeling and visualization of large multidomain proteins. The tool was implemented in Python and is fully integrated into the software, which can be downloaded here.

MDA provides molecular biologists with an overview of structural homologues of a target protein and provide guidance for finding the best template structures for homology modeling. If you want to know more about MDA, have a look at the corresponding publication. The manual for using the software can be found here. The image illustrates how starting from a protein sequence, MDA searches for template structures including co-complexed structures (purple ribbons), and thereby highlights mutated residues in orange. Residues with no structural coverage (gaps) are represented as grey spheres. These template structures are then used to construct a full-length model, shown by the white space-filling model in the lower left corner.
